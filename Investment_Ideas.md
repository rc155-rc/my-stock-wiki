# Investment Ideas: The Hidden Gems of the AI Supply Chain

Based on a comprehensive scan of the ecosystem graphs for [[AAPL]], [[NVDA]], [[TSM]], and [[GOOGL]], the following companies appear frequently as critical dependencies but currently lack dedicated deep-dive profiles in the vault. These are the "Hidden Gems" that power the industry leaders.

## 1. [[AMD]] (Advanced Micro Devices)
- **Context:** Mentioned as a primary competitor to [[NVDA]] in AI accelerators and [[INTC]] in CPUs.
- **Investment Thesis:** As hyperscalers ([[GOOGL]], [[MSFT]], [[AMZN]]) seek to reduce their total cost of ownership (TCO) and dependency on NVIDIA's Blackwell, AMD's MI300/MI400 series represents the most viable high-performance alternative. AMD is the "second-source" beneficiary of the AI infrastructure boom.

## 2. [[MU]] (Micron Technology)
- **Context:** Cited by [[AAPL]] and [[NVDA]] as a key memory supplier (NAND/DRAM/HBM).
- **Investment Thesis:** AI compute is useless without High-Bandwidth Memory (HBM). Apple's CFO explicitly noted rising memory prices as a headwind for their margins—which is a direct tailwind for Micron. Micron is capturing the "commodity-to-specialty" pivot as HBM becomes a high-margin bottleneck.

## 3. [[AMZN]] (Amazon / AWS)
- **Context:** A major customer for [[NVDA]] and [[AVGO]], and a competitor to [[GOOGL]] Cloud.
- **Investment Thesis:** Amazon is vertically integrating its AI stack with custom "Trainium" and "Inferentia" chips (supported by [[AVGO]] and [[TSM]]). As the world's largest cloud provider, AWS has the unique ability to force-multiply its own silicon, potentially improving its cloud margins relative to peers who rely solely on third-party hardware.

## 4. [[META]] (Meta Platforms)
- **Context:** One of the largest buyers of [[NVDA]] GPUs and a critical competitor in the "consumer AI platform" space.
- **Investment Thesis:** Meta's "Llama" models are the open-source backbone of much of the AI industry. By driving the industry toward open-source standards, Meta commoditizes the software layer, forcing value to stay at the hardware layer where they have massive scale. They are the primary beneficiary of the hardware build-out for social/ad recommendation engines.

## 5. [[SSNLF]] (Samsung Electronics)
- **Context:** A major competitor to [[AAPL]] in smartphones and [[TSM]] in foundry services, and a key supplier of HBM.
- **Investment Thesis:** Samsung is the only company that competes across the entire stack: Memory, Foundry, and Consumer Devices. If [[TSM]] capacity remains fully booked, Samsung's "alternate foundry" status becomes a massive strategic asset for companies like [[NVDA]] looking for supply diversification.
